Black pigments have been used for centuries to create the color black in art and other applications. One way to demonstrate how black is made is by combining all three primary colors (red, yellow, and blue) using liquid paint or food coloring. This simple experiment can help show that by mixing all the colors together, a dark shade that appears black can be created.

To make black paint, sources such as smoke, combustion, and minerals are used. Lamp black is made from burning oils or resins, carbon black from petroleum combustion, and iron oxide is a naturally occurring mineral. The combination of these materials is chosen to produce desired properties for the paint's intended use.
